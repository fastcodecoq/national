AL Lapinskas
CEO

Al Lapinskas started National Luxury Coach in 2013. Mr.Lapinskas is CDL

certified with a perfect driving record and demands the same from his entire staff. At the age of 21 Mr. Lapinskas graduated from The Delta Connection Academy at the top of his class. He soon went on to fly for a major airline carrier here in the United States and became one of the youngest B747 International Captains in the aviation field. Mr. Lapinskas is a highly determined individual  who is highly respected by all of his colleagues. 

Mr. Lapinskas is applying all the leadership and safety skills he has acquired in his 10 years of aviation training to the busing industry. He expects nothing less than perfection when it comes to customer satisfaction, safety and reliability from his staff. Mr.Lapinskas guarantees to hold National Luxury Coach to the highest standards in the busing industry.


Egidius Lapinskas
 Senior Driver

Egidius has 33 plus years of driving experience, with a perfect driving record. Egidius was born and raised in Lithuania and moved his family to the states 1989. He is the proud father of nine beautiful children and is very family oriented.

Egidius has worked as a senior driver for many reputable busing companies. While working for these other companies, Mr. Lapinskas was in charge of training the driving staff. Throughout his career he has been a very punctual and hard-working and is a well-respected name in the transportation industry.

National Luxury Coach drivers are all well trained and are the best drivers in the business. They are dedicated to ensure that your trip will be safe and flawless. We here at National Luxury Coach hold our drivers to the highest standards in the transportation industry.

National Luxury Coach drivers are all required to have;

  • Current and valid CDL license, with passenger endorsement
  • Current DOT physical examination
  • Pass an annual Driving Skills test
  • Comply with National Luxury Coach and DOT Drug and Alcohol Program
  • Pass a criminal background check
  • Follow all DOT rules and regulations which limits all drivers to10 hours of driving and a total of 15 hours on duty
